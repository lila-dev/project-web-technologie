About this Course

Business intelligence (BI) or decision support system (DSS) is information technology for decision-makers and business leaders. It designates the means, the tools and the methods which make it possible to collect, consolidate, model and restore the data, material or immaterial, of a company in order to offer a decision-making aid and to allow a decision-maker to have an overview of the activity processed. This type of application is based on a common architecture whose theoretical bases come mainly from Ralph Kimball, Bill Inmon and Dan Linstedt. Business intelligence fits into the broader architecture of an information system, but is not a competing concept of information system management. Just as management comes from sociology and economics, IT-based management is part of two radically different fields: management and IT. In order to enrich the concept with these two ways of thinking, it is possible to consider an IT engineering-oriented side called business intelligence, and another side serving more particularly management approaches called system management. of information.
